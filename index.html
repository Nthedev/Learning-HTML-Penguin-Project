General info

What is a Penguin?

Penguins are black and white birds.


Where do they live?

They live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere


Food

  • krill
  • fish
  • squid

They catch food and swallow it whole while swimming. A spiny tounge and powerful jaw help them grip slippery pray.


Interesting Species

  • emperor penguin (Aptenodytes forsteri) - this is the largest penguin. It weighs 35 kg (77 lb) and is 1.1m tall
  • little blue penguin (Eudyptula minor)

In total there are six genera and eighteen species


Conservation Status

The majority of living penguin species have declining populations. According to the IUCN Red List, their conservation statuses range from Least Concern through to Endangered.


Fun Facts

  • Some prehistoric penguin species were enormous: as tall or heavy as an adult human.
  • larger penguins generally inhabit colder regions, and smaller penguins inhabit regions with temperate or tropical climates
